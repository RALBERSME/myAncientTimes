12th Temple: Sphinx of Gizeh

- refers to the 10th chacra

Coordinates: 29° 58′ 31″ N, 31° 8′ 15″ O

The Sphinx was built around 2500 BC next to the Valley Temple of Chephren. The statue, which is over 70 meters long and around 20 meters high, is said to represent King Chephren as the earthly form of the sun god. It guards the pyramid field from intruders and evil spirits.

The Sphinx is the largest sculpture erected in antiquity. Some researchers believe that the figure represents the pharaoh Chephren himself. Others think that the sculpture is the sun god Re.

The name Sphinx is the name of a creature in Greek mythology, depicted as a winged lion with the upper body of a woman, who lives near Thebes and kills anyone who cannot solve the riddle it poses.
