Astrology & Astronomy in Ancient Egypt



By the early second century BC, Babylonian astrology and astronomy had spread to Egypt.
Like their Babylonian counterparts, Egyptian astrologers began to create horoscopes to predict the fate of a newborn.

Astronomy played a significant role in determining the dates of religious festivals and the hours of the night, and temple astrologers were particularly adept at observing the stars and observing the conjunctions and risings of the sun, moon, and planets, as well as the phases of the moon. The Egyptians oriented their pyramids and temples to the north because they believed their pharaohs would become stars in the northern sky after their deaths. To ensure that a king joined the circumpolar stars, the pyramids were oriented due north, toward the "indestructible" stars.

Astronomy was the first, as it has pursued the goal of understanding the cosmos through scientific observation and analysis for thousands of years. Astrology, with its emphasis on the influence of the heavens on human affairs, later developed as a belief system
