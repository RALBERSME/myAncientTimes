Saqqara is an important ancient Egyptian necropolis on the western bank of the Nile. It has been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1979 as part of the Memphis and its Necropolis site - the pyramid fields from Giza to Dahshur. The site is located about 20 km south of Cairo in the governorate of al-Giza.

The first pyramid in Egypt was built in Saqqara around 2650 BC: the step pyramid of King Djoser (3rd dynasty), which is now considered the oldest monumental stone building in Egypt.
