Coordinates: 22° 21′ N, 31° 37′ O

The temples of Abu Simbel are two rock temples on the western shore of Lake Nasser. They are located in the Egyptian part of Nubia on the southeastern edge of the town of Abu Simbel and were built in the 13th century BC under King Ramses II of the 19th dynasty of the ancient Egyptian New Kingdom. Between 1260 and 1250 BC, Pharaoh Ramses II had the gigantic structure carved into the rock for himself and the ancient Egyptian deities Re-Harakhte, Amun-Re and Ptah.

The larger building was dedicated to the king himself, the smaller, 150 meters away, to the goddess Hathor and the memory of Ramses' favorite wife Nefertari. The temples of Abu Simbel are aligned with the sun

To prevent the complex from sinking into Lake Nasser, the reservoir of the Aswan Dam, the entire complex was moved to higher ground in 1968.
