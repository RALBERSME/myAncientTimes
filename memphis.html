Coordinates: 29° 51′ N, 31° 15′ O

Memphis was the capital of the first nome of Lower Egypt. Its ruins are located near the towns of Mit-Rahineh and Helwan, about 18 km south of Cairo, and have been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1979 as part of the Memphis and its Necropolis site - the pyramid fields from Giza to Dahshur.

Most of the pyramids found near Cairo, well over 100 in total, were built during this period when Memphis was the most important city in Egypt and most likely in the world.

According to legend, Memphis was founded by King Menes in 3000 BC. During the Old Kingdom it was the capital of Egypt.
