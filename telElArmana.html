Coordinates: 27° 39′ 42″ N, 30° 54′ 20″ O

Tell el-Amarna is a major archaeological site in Middle Egypt. It contains the remains of Akhet-Aton, a short-lived capital of Ancient Egypt founded by Pharaoh Akhenaten around 1350 BC.

Tell el-Amarna is not only known for its well-preserved urban remains, but also for the Amarna Letters, an important archive of diplomatic correspondence, as well as for the revolutionary Amarna art, which represents a clear break with earlier Egyptian artistic traditions.
