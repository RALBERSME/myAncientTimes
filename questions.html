Many questions about ancient Egypt remain unanswered



Look, you are not the only one who has a lot of things that remain a mystery. Even the wisest people have not been able to solve every mystery. Despite the long history of research, there are still MANY unanswered questions about Ancient Egypt. The main question remains unchanged:

« How were the giant pyramids built? »

But there are still further questions, e.g.:

Where is the tomb of Nefertiti?

Tutankhamun died with a fractured skull and a broken knee. What happened to him?

Dozens of written records mention the land of Punt, but to this day no one knows where this land was located. Punt is often portrayed as a powerful African kingdom that existed somewhere in the south alongside the Egyptians. The Egyptians obviously respected and revered this kingdom and even called it "God's Land". But where can you find it?

There is a considerable amount of archaeological and historical evidence to suggest that a huge labyrinth once stretched out near the pyramids. The Greek historian and geographer Herodotus also mentioned a huge labyrinth near the pyramids during his visit to Egypt in the 4th century BC. Does it really exist? And if so, how could one find it?

The Pyramid of Djedefre was supposedly the most impressive pyramid ever built. Some historians even claim that it could have been as large as one of the pyramids at Giza. The Pyramid of Djedefre was destroyed for unknown reasons. Why? What happened?
Some believe that the Romans used the pyramid's stone for their own infrastructure projects, while others believe that there may have been a revolt by the Egyptian people against Djedfre and they destroyed his monument out of malice. But is this true?

In 2015, archaeologists were able to uncover the tomb of an unknown woman who was buried sometime during the Old Kingdom, about 4,500 years ago. The hieroglyphs on her sarcophagus describe her as both the "mother" and "wife" of the king.
A closer look reveals that she may have been one of the most important female figures of ancient Egypt, but aside from the discovery of the tomb, it's a complete mystery. So who was she?

In 2017, researchers studied the largest pyramid, Cheops, and their findings were simply incredible. In addition to the three chambers discovered in previous years, there was a much larger hidden chamber at the base of the pyramid. The chamber has no paths or known entrances. What was it for? Why was it created?

The origin of the Sphinx is completely unknown. It was once thought that the Egyptians built the Sphinx like the pyramids, but this may not be correct.
Water erosion on the base of the statue dates the Sphinx to around 7000 BC. This would suggest that either ancient Egypt is much older than initially thought or that an earlier, equally advanced civilization built the Sphinx. Who built the Sphinx, when, and for what purpose?

The Dendera Light is an enigmatic inscription carved into the walls of a temple dedicated to the Egyptian goddess Hathor. The image appears to depict either the use or instructions for making a Crookes tube, an item used as a light bulb in the 19th century. Had the Egyptians already developed a simple form of electricity?
