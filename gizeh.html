14th Temple: King's Chamber in the Great Pyramid of Giza

- refers to the 12th chacra / Crown chacra

Coordinates: 29° 58′ 45″ N, 31° 8′ 3″ O

The Great Pyramid of Cheops is the oldest and largest of the three pyramids of Giza and is therefore also known as the "Great Pyramid". The highest pyramid in the world was built as a tomb for the Egyptian King Cheops, who ruled during the 4th Dynasty in the Old Kingdom.

It was built between about 2550 and 2490 BC.

Its original side length is calculated to be 230.33 m and its height 146.59 m (about 280 cubits). This made it the tallest building in the world for around four thousand years. Since it was later used as a quarry, its height today is still 138.75 m.
