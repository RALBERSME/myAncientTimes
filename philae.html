The Temple of Philae is a temple complex in Upper Egypt, about eight kilometers south of Aswan. It was completed by Ptolemy II Philadelphus (reigned 285-246 BC) and his successor Ptolemy III Euergetes (reigned 246-221 BC).

Legend has it that Philae is the place where the goddess Isis found the heart of her husband Osiris after he was killed and dismembered by his brother Seth.
