The most important Gods in Acient Egypt





In Egyptian mythology there were about 1,500 gods. The most important gods in ancient Egypt (3032 BC to 395 AD) were:

Ra (Re) - the sun god
The earliest gods, Ra and/or Atum (both creator/sun gods), arose from a chaotic state of the world and gave birth to Shu (air) and Tefnut (moisture), whose union gave birth to Geb (earth) and Nut (sky), who in turn created Osiris, Isis, Set and Nephthys. In his human form, Ra is usually depicted as a falcon-headed man wearing a red disk on his head, representing the sun that Ra created at the beginning of the world.

Re (Ra) Egyptian god / Britannica
The Sun God Ra supreme power in the universe Kemetic Egyptian Mythology




Osiris - the god of the underworld and judge of the dead. He was therefore the god of the afterlife and judge of the court of the dead. He was the brother of Seth, Nephthys and Isis. His main task was to decide as judge of the court of the dead whether the dead were allowed to enter the afterlife. The body of Osiris is usually depicted as a mummy, on his head he wears a crown with a feather on each side. In his hands he holds the royal symbols of a crosier and a fan. According to the myth, Osiris was killed by his brother Seth out of jealousy. Seth dismembered the body and scattered it all over Egypt. His sister-wife Isis found its individual parts, put it back together and brought it back to life

Osiris Egyptian god / Britannica




Isis - the goddess of birth; she is the sister and wife of Osiris. She was the goddess of birth, rebirth and magic. In the Greco-Roman cult at the latest, she also became the mistress of the underworld, who was involved in rites for the dead.Osiris and Isis are twins who fell in love in the womb and grew up as a "couple whose love surpasses all understanding." But a jealous brother murdered Osiris and dismembered his body into 14 pieces. Isis succeeds in saving her beloved Osiris, at least for the afterlife, where he is given a new task. He becomes the king of the underworld, called "Duat" in Egyptian mythology, and the ruler of the blessed dead. Isis is depicted in human form as a woman. On her head she carries a throne - the hieroglyph of her name. In many depictions her headdress also consists of a cow's horn with a sun disk.

Isis Egyptian goddess / Britannica




Horus - the lord of the world; he is the son of Osiris and Isis. In the early days of ancient Egyptian religion, Horus was considered the god of war and the sky and was married to the goddess Hathor. As the religion developed, Horus was seen as the son of Osiris and Isis and as the opponent of Seth. He channels enormous amounts of solar energy through his ankh talisman or bladed staff. His left eye, known as the "Eye of Horus," can paralyze those in his immediate vicinity. Horus can also take the physical form of a falcon or a falcon-headed humanoid. According to Egyptian mythology, Horus lost his left eye in battle with Seth. The eye was magically restored by Hathor, and this restoration symbolized the process of restoration and healing. For this reason, the symbol was often used in amulets.



Seth - the desert god and companion of Osiris.
Seth (also Set, Setech, Sutech; variant Wedja) is an ambivalent ancient Egyptian deity whose meaning is not entirely clear. Seth is a desert god and is associated with storms and bad weather, which is why he is considered the god of chaos and destruction.In other words he was the god of violence, chaos and confusion, the evil god who also embodied anger, rage, violence and murder. He was a trickster, a sky god, lord of the desert and master of storms, disorder and war. He was the brother of Osiris, whom he killed, and he was an opponent of Horus, the child of Osiris' sister Isis.

Seth Egyptian god / Britannica




Anubis, like Osiris, was one of the gods of the dead. He was part of the court of the dead and was responsible for mummification and conducting funeral rites. He was closely connected to Osiris and was even portrayed as his son in some myths. Anubis is mainly depicted as a lying black dog, jackal or as a human with a dog or jackal head. He usually carries a was-scepter and an ankh. Rarely, he is also depicted with a ram's head. In ancient Egypt, animals were highly valued because they were considered a connection to the divine sphere.
