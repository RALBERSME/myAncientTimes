- refers to the 4th chacra - male -

Coordinates: 25° 43′ N, 32° 39′ O

The Karnak Temples are the largest temple complex in Egypt and are located in Karnak, a village about 2.5 kilometers north of Luxor and directly on the eastern bank of the Nile. The oldest remains of the temple that are still visible today date from the 12th Dynasty under Sesostris I.

The purpose of the temples was to maintain Maat, because the ancient Egyptian belief system was based on the principle of cosmological order. This principle was also called Maat.

The temple dates from 2055 BC to about 100 AD.

Karnak is an open-air museum. It is considered the largest religious building or site in the world. The 54,000 square meter Great Hypostyle Hall in the Karnak Temple is large enough to fit comfortably in Notre Dame Cathedral.
