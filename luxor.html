Coordinates: 25° 42′ N, 32° 39′ O

Luxor is the ancient city of the pharaohs. It was once the capital of Egypt and the spiritual and religious center.

Luxor was built in the period of the "New Kingdom" between 1550-1060/70 BC.

The Temple of Luxor is famous, a temple in honor of the god Amun, which was largely built by Amenhotep III. One of the granite obelisks that was erected in front of the temple now stands on the Place de la Concorde in Paris (see Obelisk of Luxor).
