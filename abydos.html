Abydos is an archaeological site near Sohag on the western bank of the Nile, 160 kilometers north of Luxor and about 15 kilometers southwest of the modern-day city of el-Balyana. In ancient times, the site was assigned to the 8th Upper Egyptian Ta-wer-Nomad and was one of the most important necropolises in the country.

The temple was built in the 13th century (1303–1213 BC).

Abydos is an ancient and mysterious necropolis in Upper Egypt. It was already old before the first pharaohs ruled over Egypt. Princes from ancient times had chosen this holy place to find their final resting place. During the time of the pharaohs, Abydos became a place of pilgrimage.
